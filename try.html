About

Inspiration to innovation.

Every year about 1.5 lakh young boys and girls join in one or other of the city's coaching institutes to participate in the race of scoring high in JEE and get their favourite course in favorite college but only few hundred succeed, rest of them remain in dilemma which college to choose.

A student has to pay as much as Rs 1 lakh tuition fee and an equal amount on lodging and boarding. It makes the coaching market of Kota worth Rs 300 crore. As such, unlike traditional tuition centres, top institutes not only look, behave and have a corporate approach but are engaged in cutthroat competition.

As the competition to grab a seat in the best institutes is on the rise so are the suicidal tendencies among students. The town has 11 psychiatrists, who attend at least five children daily on average. In most cases, these students were toppers in their schools, villages and states. But when they come here, amidst a line of toppers from across India, their rank falls. "At the back of a student's mind is the fear of being a failure and all the money that his family has spent on him," says Sushil. The root cause of distress among the students was found to be the fear of failure and loss of the money which family has spent on them.

   Piyush, a teacher at Allen Career Institute, wanted to help these children. He has figured out a way to help them. He has planned to start selling notebooks and books at highly subsidized cost by inserting advertisements into it. The advertisements would be kept strictly to the business which would not distract students from studying but would help them explore tools which would assist them. The design of the notebooks would be centred around Hope. There will be motivational quotes and stories in them so that students compete with oneself and acquire excellence instead of competing with others.
